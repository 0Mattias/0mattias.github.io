WhalEmu, in miniature

A teaching-sized slice of a private project · runs entirely in your browser

WhalEmu is a real-mode x86 CPU emulator with a browser-based step debugger. The full version stays private; this page is a small working slice of the idea: a two-pass assembler, eight 16-bit registers, four flags, a visible stack, and two dozen instructions. Load a sample or write your own, then step through it and watch the registers move.

Pick a sample or write your own, then Assemble.

    Registers AX BX CX DX SI DI BP SP, all 16-bit. Flags ZF SF CF OF. Immediates in decimal, 0x hex, or trailing-h hex. Labels end with a colon, comments start with a semicolon. OUT prints a register to the output panel, standing in for the DOS print interrupt. The instruction listing executes by index rather than from encoded bytes; the full emulator does real instruction encoding, this page keeps the part that teaches.
